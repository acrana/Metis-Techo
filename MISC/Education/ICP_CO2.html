  • EVD (External Ventricular Drain)
                  
    Inserts a catheter to drain CSF, reducing ICP rapidly (e.g., 5–15 mmHg drop). Used when ICP > 20 mmHg with hydrocephalus or poor compliance.
    
                  
    Watch for: Infection (5–20% risk), over-drainage (subdural hematoma), catheter blockage.
    
                
    • 
                
  • Mannitol
                  
    Osmotic diuretic (0.25–1 g/kg IV), lowers ICP within 15–30 min, lasting 1–6 hours. Effective for edema-related ICP spikes.
    
                  
    Watch for: Dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, renal failure (if overused), rebound ICP rise.
    
                
    • 
                
  • Craniotomy/Decompressive Craniectomy
                  
    Surgical removal of skull section to allow brain expansion, used in refractory ICP > 40 mmHg (e.g., herniation risk).
    
                  
    Watch for: Bleeding, infection, paradoxical herniation, long-term deficits.
    
                
    • 
                
  • Sedation/Barbiturates
                  
    Reduces metabolic demand and ICP (e.g., propofol, pentobarbital). Used when other measures fail.
    
                  
    Watch for: Hypotension, prolonged recovery, immunosuppression.
    
                
    • 
              

              
Tip: Combine with CO₂ management—hyperventilation is temporizing; these address underlying causes.
